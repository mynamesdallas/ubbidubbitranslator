What is Pig-Latin?

Pig Latin is a playful, secret language game where words are altered by moving the initial consonant or consonant cluster to the end and adding '-ay', or by just adding '-ay' or '-way' if the word starts with a vowel.

Learn the language rules

  • If a word starts with a consonant: Move the first consonant (or consonant cluster, like "sh", "th") to the end and add "-ay".
    1. Example: "dog" becomes "og-day", "friend" becomes "end-fray".
  • If a word starts with a vowel: Add "-ay", "-yay", or "-way" to the end.
    1. Example: "apple" becomes "apple-way", "out" becomes "out-yay".
